Project Overview-

This repository contains the institutional-grade equity research initiation report and valuation architecture for Delhivery Ltd., prepared for the annual CFA Institute Research Challenge. 
Delhivery operates as a fully integrated, technology-driven logistics provider in India across Express Parcel, Part-Truckload, Full-Truckload, Supply Chain Services, and Cross-Border logistics. 
Following deep financial forensics, macroeconomic mapping, and evaluation of corporate governance frameworks, our team initiated a high-conviction SELL recommendation on the stock.

Market optimism has baked in overly aggressive growth targets that leave no margin of safety for incoming structural ecosystem risks.
High Capital Expenditure Pressures: Sustaining growth requires constant capital deployment that aggressively dwarfs baseline asset depreciation, with Capex at Rupee 601 Crore versus Depreciation at Rupee 462 Crore in FY25. This structural dynamic caps free cash flow expansion despite achieving net accounting profitability. 
E-Commerce Client Insourcing Counter-Winds: Major anchor clients like Meesho are systematically expanding internal, captive logistics infrastructures. 
This insourcing trend is expected to peak near 70% in FY26E, taking away a massive chunk of outsource volumes from independent express carriers. 
Quick Commerce Disruptions: The consumer shift toward Quick Commerce platforms is anticipated to hit its peak disruptive channel effect in FY26, pulling volume market share away from traditional horizontal e-commerce marketplaces. 
Tech Story versus Financial Disclosures: While positioning itself as a tech-first pioneer, Delhivery reports no distinct R&D expense line items inside its financial disclosures and filed zero active patents during its IPO process, undermining its premium proprietary innovation narrative.  

Analytical Frameworks and Appendix Architecture-

Appendix I: Business and Industry Analysis (Porter's 5 Forces)Bargaining Power of Buyers scored 5 out of 5: Extremely high.
High buyer concentration and low switching costs give customers immense pricing power, directly compressing margin expansion.  
Bargaining Power of Suppliers scored 3 out of 5: Moderate to high.
Driven by specialized fuel inputs, localized fleet networks, and partner dependence.
Threat of New Entrants scored 4 out of 5: Elevated due to asset-light, platform-based entry paths, though muted slightly by Delhivery’s automated sorting infrastructure network. 
Threat of Substitutes scored 3 out of 5: Impacted heavily by quick commerce platforms and direct client captive operations. 
Industry Rivalry scored 2.5 out of 5: Consolidating into a visible structural duopoly between Delhivery and Shadowfax, though intense price wars remain across legacy B2B segments. 

Appendix II: Financial Statements and Forensic Health
The model features dynamic forward projections for FY26E to FY30E rooted in historical tracking from FY24A to FY25A.
Revenue projections are calculated to scale from Rupee 8,142 Crore in FY24A to Rupee 14,386 Crore by FY30E. 
Adjusted Net Profit is charted turning positive at Rupee 162 Crore in FY25A and is projected to climb linearly to Rupee 861 Crore by FY30E.
The Piotroski F-Score of 5 out of 9 confirms an economically sound baseline operational posture, but tracks long-term leverage increases and stagnant current asset ratios.
The Beneish M-Score of -2.70 safely falls below the critical threshold of -1.78, indicating a low probability of financial statement manipulation.  

Appendix III: Corporate Governance and ESG Risk MatrixEnvironmental Evaluation (Medium Risk): Disclosures remain purely qualitative without time-bound carbon emission targets.
Minor green steps like 20 LNG trucks and limited solar tie-ups are mathematically small relative to total fleet intensity.  
Social Capital Evaluation (High Risk): Historical tracking shows workplace complaints, with 26 sexual harassment cases reported in FY22.
High internal attrition and structural reliance on third-party contractual labor pose risks of operational volatility and labor cost inflation.  
Governance Deep Dive (High Risk): The corporate structure lacks an explicit, identifiable promoter, opening up accountability risks. 
Auditor comments in FY22 and FY23 noted missing local server backups and audit trail compliance issues. Continuous related-party transactions, such as Rupee 328.6 Million in services sold to management-linked entities in FY25, require strict oversight.  

Appendix IV: Valuation ArchitectureEarnings Multiplier Methodology: Modeled using an active TTM EPS of Rupee 2.62 multiplied by targeted industry peer horizons. 
The Conservative scenario at 35x implies an intrinsic value of Rupee 91.70.
The Moderate scenario at 40x implies an intrinsic value of Rupee 104.80. 
The Optimistic scenario at 45x implies an intrinsic value of Rupee 117.90. 
Relative Peer Multiples Benchmarking: Assessed against a standard peer basket of 9 listed domestic operators including Blue Dart, Container Corp, TVS Supply Chain, and Transport Corp.
Applying the peer median trailing EV/EBITDA multiple of 13.94x yields a fair price of Rupee 151.44, while the peer median P/E multiple of 26.52x drops the fair price calculation down to Rupee 70.68.
